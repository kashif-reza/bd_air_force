Mission & Overview

The Bangladesh Air Force (BAF) stands as the formidable aerial warfare branch of the Bangladesh Armed Forces. Its crucial mission encompasses safeguarding the nation's airspace, providing essential air support to ground and naval operations, and actively participating in vital humanitarian aid and international peacekeeping efforts.

Since its heroic inception in 1971, the BAF has evolved into a modern, highly disciplined, and technologically advanced force, relentlessly dedicated to defending sovereignty and contributing to global stability.

The Legend of "Kilo Flight"

The BAF's origin is deeply intertwined with the 1971 War of Liberation. A small group of defecting Bengali pilots and technicians formed "Kilo Flight". Operating against overwhelming odds, they commenced operations with an incredibly limited fleet:

  • DC-3 Dakota (Transport/Bomber)
  • DHC-3 Otter (Utility)
  • Alouette III (Helicopter)

Established: September 28, 1971 (Air Force Day)
